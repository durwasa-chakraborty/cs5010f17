Step 3: Method Design

  • Each method definition should have a contract that is the same as the contract in the interface. (In Java, the compiler enforces this.)
  • A method may have a purpose statement that specializes the purpose statement given in the interface.
  • Methods should have examples as needed to clarify the purpose statement.
  • Each method should have tests associated with it.
  • Document your method with a strategy if that would help readers to understand the definition.
  • Document your method with a halting measure if it is recursive and the halting measure isn't obvious.
