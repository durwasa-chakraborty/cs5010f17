Optimizations that Improve Asymptotic Efficiency

Although most compiler optimizations improve efficiency by only a small constant factor, some optimizations may improve the asymptotic efficiency of a program.

We saw that with my benchmark program, where the compiler's removal of an entire timing loop reduced the running time from Θ(m n) to Θ(n).

Even without proper tail recursion, tail call optimization can reduce the space used by a program from Ω(n) to O(1).
