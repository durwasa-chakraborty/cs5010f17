Generational Garbage Collection

Generational garbage collectors allocate all small objects in a region of memory called the nursery. When the nursery fills up, they move reachable objects out of the nursery into another area of memory, reclaiming the entire nursery all at once.

Those minor collections take time proportional to the reachable storage within the nursery, because the collector touches only the reachable objects.

Explicit deallocation touches all of the objects whose storage is being reclaimed, so explicit deallocation takes time proportional to the storage that is about to become unreachable.

With our naive micro-benchmark, the reachable storage averages about 500 objects, which occupy a very small fraction of the nursery. If the nursery is large enough to hold a million list nodes, which is fairly typical, the garbage collector touches only 1 out of every 2000 objects that are allocated.

Explicit deallocation has to touch every object that's allocated. That's the main reason why it runs so much slower on this micro-benchmark.
