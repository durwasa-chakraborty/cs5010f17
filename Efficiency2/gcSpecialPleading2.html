Special Pleading

  • These benchmarks are unfair because malloc/free and new/delete, as implemented in C and C++, are very slow.

Yes, they're slow. That's sort of the point.

Other allocators and deallocators are available, just as other garbage collectors are available. In these benchmarks, we timed the default allocator and deallocator used by gcc, just as we timed the default garbage collectors used by Oracle's JVM and by Larceny.

On one benchmark, we also timed Larceny's non-generational collector. We did that primarily to show how that one benchmark was designed to make generational garbage collectors perform poorly.

It came as a surprise to me that the generational collectors often out-performed explicit deallocation even on a benchmark that had been designed to make generational collectors look bad.

It is entirely possible that alternative allocators and deallocators for C and C++ might perform better, on at least some of these benchmarks, than the default implementations we timed. In like manner, all of the garbage collectors we timed can be tuned to perform better on specific benchmarks.
