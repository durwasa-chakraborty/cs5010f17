Summary

  • If someone asks you which is faster, garbage collection or explicit deallocation, all correct answers will say it depends on the program, the inputs to the program, the allocator/deallocator or garbage collector, and the parameters used by the storage management system.
  • Explicit deallocation often uses less space than garbage collection.
  • Garbage collectors tend to run faster when allowed to use more space. Some garbage collectors use space aggressively as a matter of course, and may need to be restrained.
  • Garbage collectors can take advantage of multiple processor cores even when the program itself is single-threaded.
  • Precise generational collectors usually touch less storage than conservative collectors or explicit deallocators. This is probably the main reason why garbage collection often runs faster than explicit deallocation.
