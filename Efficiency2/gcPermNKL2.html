The MPermNKL Benchmark

The GCBench benchmark was designed to be representative of real applications.

The MPermNKL benchmark was designed to be a near-worst-case for generational garbage collection.

As mentioned above, this benchmark was designed as a near-worst-case for generational garbage collection. That is one of the reasons why Larceny's non-generational stop-and-copy collector usually performs better than the other systems tested, except on the fastest machines where Java's ability to exploit extra processor cores becomes more important than generational versus non-generational collection.

Another reason is that Larceny's non-generational collector was allowed to use more memory than Larceny's generational collector, but not as much memory as Java uses by default.
